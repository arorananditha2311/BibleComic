Genesis Chapter 11

In Genesis 11, the people of the earth, united by one language, come together to build a city and a great tower that reaches the heavens—the Tower of Babel. Their pride and desire to make a name for themselves leads them away from God’s will.

In response, God confuses their language, scattering them across the earth and halting the construction of the tower. This event marks the origin of different languages and nations. The chapter also records the genealogy from Shem to Abram, setting the stage for God’s covenant with Abram in the next chapters.

The story of Babel reminds us of the importance of humility and unity in God’s plan, showing that human pride cannot stand against His will.
